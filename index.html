About

BLAH BLAH BIER (in short, BIER) is a one-week hackathon co-organized by Rostlab, Technische Universität München, DBCLS, and tagtog. The BIER hackathon is a sister event of the BLAH events.

The goal is to improve the interoperability of biomedical text mining tools and make them truly useful for final users. We invite annotation tools, developers of text mining methods, and, specially, public biomedical databases and projects. We want independent groups to hack together practical solutions, make their tools and resources speak with each other, and complete real uses cases for the benefit of the biomedical community at large. In particular, we focus on REST APIs and exchange formats, and receive feedback from the final biomedical users to improve those.

In case you wonder, BIER stands for Biomedical Interoperable and Exchangeable Resources. It also happens to be a good acronym for tasting together good German bier at the #Oktoberfest. The BIER hackathon is joyfully hosted by the Rostlab group, in Munich, the week of September 19-23. Join us!

Program

The main objective is to further formalize the interfaces of the PubAnnotation resource of linked annotations, the exchange protocol and format REST-OA, and the annotation tools and methods that produce the annotations. We want to improve the APIs together with the final users.

Confirmed annotation tools and methods are:

  • brat
  • JensenLab extraction methods
  • Hypothes.is
  • tagtog

Confirmed biomedical databases and projects are:

  • Aquaria
  • DisGeNET
  • HIVmut

We will specify the exact week schedule as the date approaches. So far it's established that:

  • It will be primarily a full hackathon to get things done, with one day for keynotes and presentations.
  • Prof. Dr. Burkhard Rost will give a keynote. We have more keynote speakers to be confirmed.
  • On Tuesday 20th we will dine together at the Oktoberfest, 2016.
  • We will publish a joint paper, likely at BMC Bioinformatics.
