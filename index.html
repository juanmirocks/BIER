About

#BLAHmuc is a one-week hackathon plus symposium co-organized by Rostlab, Technische Universität München, DBCLS, and tagtog. #BLAHmuc (BLAH+muc) is a sister event of BLAH (Biomedical Linked Annotation Hackathon), hosted in Munich (muc; city airport 3-letter code).

Our goal is to improve the interoperability of biomedical text mining systems and make them readily usable for final users. We invite annotation tools, developers of text mining methods, and, specially, public biomedical databases and other projects. We want independent groups to hack together practical solutions, make their tools and resources speak with each other, and complete real uses cases for the benefit of the biomedical community at large. In particular, we focus on formalizing REST APIs and exchange formats, and receive feedback from the final biomedical users to improve those. Together.

Questions that will be asked are: what exchange format should everybody support, JSON-LD / REST-OA? PubAnnotation format? BioC?; what should be the common REST parameters in API calls? how to handle computer-intensive queries? user auth?; what licenses for shared annotations and methods?; how to exchange information for full-text articles?; what is the opinion of final users?

Call for Applications

We ask participants to submit by July 6th July 28th a 1-page description of the project they want to present or accomplish in the #BLAHmuc hackathon. If you cannot submit an application on time, but intend to participate, please contact us without delay.

Multiple authors can send a joint submission and at least one of the authors should participate in the hackathon. At the symposium on Monday 19th, event participants will present their project and goals in a talk. Based on mutual interest and shared purposes, independent participant groups will work together to realize those. From the hackathon results, we will pursue to publish a joint paper.

Applications are to be submitted via EasyChair. We prefer webpage submissions (example from BLAH2). In that case, simply put the web link in the abstract field of the EasyChair submission form. Otherwise, please fill in the abstract field and submit your application via pdf. Submissions will be reviewed by the organizing committee.

  • Pre-confirmed annotation tools and methods: brat, JensenLab, tagtog
  • Pre-confirmed biomedical databases and projects: Aquaria, HIVmut

Social Events

We have a reception dinner on Monday 19th where will enjoy typical bavarian cuisine. We will likely go to Gasthof Neuwirt, in Garching.

On Tuesday 20th evening we go together to the Oktoberfest. We have tables reserved and both the hackathon participants and other visitors of the Rostlab group for that week are invited.

At the registration on Monday 19th, we will ask participants to pay a fee for the organization of these events. Depending on available funding, the fee will be either 3 Euros, or otherwise 35 Euros.

Hotel

We have a reserved hotel for a maximum of 20 invitees. The hotel is ideal for being located near the TUM faculty where the hackathon will take place and near the center of Munich via public transportation (map location). The price of an individual room is €199 and the price of a shared 2-bed room is €259. Due to the hectic Oktoberfest time, hotel prices are similar and higher in all Munich area. After the approval and confirmation of your participation, you will receive a special code for booking. You could seek other options for staying yourself. However, hotel rooms will be difficult to book and expensive.

Funding

You will need to book the trip and hotel yourself. After participating at the event, part of the costs will be refunded to you. We can not guarantee at the moment the quantity for reimbursement but expect to support 50% of the staying costs.
