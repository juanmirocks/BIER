Background

The goal of the BLAH hackathons is to bring together the community of biomedical text mining and its users. We discuss and find solutions to make text mining truly useful for the bioinformatics and medical spaces. For instance, we have created the PubAnnotation repository, where text annotations are contributed by independent groups and shared to all users.

The DBCLS organized in 2015 the first two BLAH hackathons near Tokyo, Japan. Well-renowned experts participated, e.g., Larry Hunter, Dietrich Rebholz-Schuhmann, or Cathy Wu, to name only a few!

This year we do it again, in #Munich! We will have the chance again to collaborate with international groups, only this time enjoying not sake but good German bier at the #Oktoberfest! Technische Universität München and the Rostlab group will host the event.

Program

The main goal of this year is to develop applications with the PubAnnotation resource and improve it with your feedback. In particular, we are inviting high-profile databases and projects to satisfy real use cases. Confirmed projects are:

  • Aquaria
  • DisGeNET
  • HIVmut

We are still specifying the exact program. So far it's estabalished that:
